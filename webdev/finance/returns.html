Stock Calculation Tips


How to calculate a company's profit margin

Profit margin is one the easiest ways to determine how a company is doing. It can be calculated very simply as well. Just divide the profit by revenue and multiply by 100. Simple!

How to calculate total company value

Total company value, also known as market capitalization, is calculated by multiplying the current stock price by the total number of outstanding shares. This gives you a quick snapshot of a company's overall value in the market.

How to calculate Earnings Per Share (EPS)

Earnings Per Share (EPS) is calculated by dividing a company's net profit by the number of outstanding shares. This metric helps investors understand how much profit is allocated to each share of stock, providing insight into the company's profitability on a per-share basis.

How to calculate Price to Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio)

The Price to Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio) is calculated by dividing the current market price of a company's stock by its Earnings Per Share (EPS). This ratio provides insight into how much investors are willing to pay for each dollar of earnings, helping to assess whether a stock is overvalued or undervalued.

How to calculate Return on Investment (ROI)

Return on Investment (ROI) is calculated by taking the net profit from an investment, dividing it by the initial cost of the investment, and then multiplying by 100 to get a percentage. This metric helps investors evaluate the efficiency and profitability of their investments.
